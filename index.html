How To Learn

The Intuitive Guide To Learning

By Andrew Wilson

What it feels like to learn to code in 2016

First of all, the programming is a mess.
When I first began to learn how to code I began by taking a course at codecademy, started with some HTML/CSS.
Cool.
Tags, attributes, selectors, hey I’m getting pretty good at this!
Cool.
Finished that, next course: Javascript.
Cool.
I did this in high-school. Loops, conditions, functions… Hey this isn’t too bad!
Finished.
I was feeling pretty good at this point…
Until I actually wanted to create something.
Funny enough I found out that this is pretty much why the industry changes so often.

Programming languages, frameworks, libraries, algorithms, data structures, databases…
And those are just the TOOLS.
Then you have authentication, sessions, cookies, caching, server requests, parsing data…
Oh. My. God.
I fell into what is known as “informed pessimism”.
How am I going to learn all of this?

Well I’ve spent a long time in the “Valley of Despair”, but what I realize about programming is that even people who have been doing this for 7 years are still learning everyday!

Learning is to be expected and it can become one of the most fulfilling and fun parts of programming.
Things chance very quickly, but thankfully, so can our brains.
And so today I will give you some resources to help you add learning to your skills.

How The Brain Learns

The brain is very complex so we are going to simplify things into two different modes: Focused and Diffused.
We will be using a pinball machine for analogy.
When you think a thought, the pinball is launched and it travels along the pathways that you’ve already laid. The stronger those pathways are the easier it is to recall that thought.
However what if you want to think a new thought that you’ve never thought of before?
At the bottom you’ll notice some black lines that represent a thought you would like to have.
How do you get to those pathways?
The diffuse mode.
In the diffuse mode of thinking you’re able think a wider range of thoughts and connect to farther apart ideas to each other.
The way you enter the diffuse mode is by turning your attention away from what you’re doing and letting your brain focus on different things.
That’s why sometimes when you’re unable to solve a problem the answer will pop into your head a bit after you’ve moved on.

How To Apply This

Stepping away from science, and back to practicality, how do you get better and get to learning all this awesome cool stuff?
Build understanding, practice, repeat. When you focus on problems you’ve solved before the process is painless and quick, you arrive at the solution without effort.
However when you’re encountering new problems, don’t get frustrated. It helps to understand the underlying concepts so don't be afraid to do some research.
Keep working through it and allow your brain to form those new pathways and you'll be picking things up way faster than you thought!

How To Learn Anything

All it takes to be good at something is 20 hours. In fact what you’ll notice is any skill is actually a set of sub-skills.
There are a ton of things you can learn and the ONLY thing stopping you is 20 hours.
Now those 20 hours are going to be confusing, frustrating perhaps, but realize that it’s just apart of the process to becoming competent.
